The Native "Lukot" / "Kiyot"

Stingless bees store honey in cerumen pots, not combs.

Forest Architects

They are major pollinators of Dipterocarp forests and fruit trees. Their small size allows them to enter flowers other bees cannot.

Pot Honey

Stored in egg-shaped pots made of beeswax and resin. This honey is more liquid, acidic, and rich in medicinal properties.

Resin Defense

Lacking a sting, they defend the colony by biting and sealing intruders in sticky resin (Propolis).

CAPSTONE 2025-2026

Audio-Video Analysis for Colony Activity and Intrusion Alert for Tetragonula biroi Utilizing Machine-Based Learning

The Challenge

Manual inspection destroys the intricate resin structures (involucrum) that T. biroi build to regulate heat. Breaking this seal stresses the colony.

The Innovation

We use Edge Machine Learning to "listen" and "watch" the hive entrance. By analyzing flight patterns and buzz frequencies, we detect stress remotely.

System Architecture

  • Video Analysis: Computer vision for traffic counting.
  • Audio Analysis: ML recognition of stress buzzing.
  • Edge ML: On-device intrusion alerts.
  • Env. Sensors: Monitoring microclimate.
